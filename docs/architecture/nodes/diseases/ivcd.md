## 1. Pathophysiology Root Cause

### 1.1 Anatomic Substrate

IVCD is a descriptive ECG term, not a specific anatomic diagnosis. It applies when ventricular conduction is slow (QRS ≥120ms) but the morphology does not satisfy established criteria for RBBB (no rSR' in V1), LBBB (no broad notched R in I/V5–V6 with loss of septal q), or a defined fascicular block. The wide QRS reflects delayed propagation of the ventricular activation wavefront through at least part of the ventricular myocardium — but the delay is non-localizable to a specific bundle branch by surface ECG morphology.

### 1.2 Cellular Mechanisms (Multiple Causes)

IVCD encompasses several fundamentally different pathophysiologic processes:

**Bilateral bundle branch disease**: disease of both the right and left bundle branches simultaneously, each partially (but not fully) blocked — the resulting QRS is wide but the morphology does not fully satisfy criteria for either BBB.

**Diffuse myocardial fibrosis or cardiomyopathy**: cell-to-cell conduction slowed diffusely due to interstitial fibrosis, replacing fast Purkinje propagation with slow myocardial propagation. Seen in dilated cardiomyopathy, ischemic cardiomyopathy, infiltrative disease (amyloid, sarcoidosis).

**Metabolic disruption — hyperkalemia**: elevated extracellular potassium depolarizes the resting membrane potential toward threshold, inactivating fast sodium channels. Conduction velocity falls dramatically. The QRS widens progressively and eventually the morphology degenerates into a sine wave pattern. Crucially, hyperkalemic QRS widening does NOT produce typical RBBB or LBBB patterns — it is the paradigm IVCD from a metabolic cause.

**Sodium channel blockade — toxic**: Class Ia antiarrhythmics (procainamide, quinidine), Class Ic antiarrhythmics (flecainide, propafenone), and tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) in overdose block fast sodium channels. QRS widens without specific BBB morphology. TCA overdose: terminal 40ms QRS vector shifts rightward (prominent R in aVR >3mm) — specific pattern within IVCD.

### 1.3 Hemodynamic Consequence

Wide QRS reduces mechanical efficiency of ventricular contraction (dyssynchrony). Severity depends on underlying etiology. In cardiomyopathy, IVCD may significantly impair EF. In hyperkalemia, the hemodynamic consequence is primarily from concurrent conduction instability (risk of VF or asystole) rather than the mechanical dyssynchrony itself. In TCA overdose, the sodium channel block also depresses myocardial contractility directly.

## 2. ECG Presentation — Lead by Lead

### 2.1 Diagnostic Criteria (2025 AHA/ESC Guidelines)

Non-specific IVCD is a diagnosis of exclusion:
1. QRS duration ≥120ms
2. Does NOT meet LBBB criteria: absence of broad, notched, or slurred R-wave in I, aVL, V5, V6 with absent septal q-waves and absent S-wave in lateral leads
3. Does NOT meet RBBB criteria: absence of secondary R-wave (rSR' or rsR') in V1, absence of wide terminal S in I and V6
4. Does NOT meet criteria for left anterior fascicular block or left posterior fascicular block combined with any bundle branch block
5. QRS morphology is atypical, fragmented, or diffusely abnormal

For incomplete IVCD: QRS 100–119ms with non-specific morphologic slurring.

### 2.2 Lead-by-Lead Manifestation

| Lead | Expected Finding | Why | Sensitivity |
|------|-----------------|-----|-------------|
| I | Wide QRS; morphology varies — may show fragmented or broadened QRS without clear BBB signature | Lateral lead; diffuse conduction delay produces non-specific widening | Moderate |
| II | Wide QRS; may show notching or slurring | Inferior lead; non-specific slowing | Moderate |
| III | Wide QRS; variable morphology | Inferior lead | Low (non-specific) |
| aVR | Terminal R-wave >3mm in aVR if TCA overdose; otherwise variable | TCA: terminal rightward QRS vector deflection reflects Na-channel block; normal QRS axis shift | High for TCA |
| aVL | Wide QRS; non-specific | Lateral augmented | Low |
| aVF | Wide QRS | Inferior augmented | Low |
| V1 | Wide QRS WITHOUT rSR' — this is critical to exclude RBBB | If there IS rSR', reclassify as RBBB | Very high (key exclusion criterion) |
| V2 | Wide QRS; may show variable morphology | Right precordial | Moderate |
| V3 | Wide QRS; variable | Mid-precordial | Low |
| V4 | Wide QRS; variable | Mid-precordial | Low |
| V5 | Wide QRS WITHOUT broad notched R (excludes LBBB) | Left precordial; if broad R present, reclassify as LBBB | Very high (key exclusion criterion) |
| V6 | Wide QRS; no dominant broad R with absent septal q (excludes LBBB) | Left precordial | Very high (key exclusion criterion) |
| All leads | QRS fragmentation (multiple notches within QRS complex) may be seen | Indicates inhomogeneous, scar-related or metabolic conduction slowing | Moderate |

### 2.3 Key Leads

- **V1**: Must confirm absence of rSR' to exclude RBBB — the most important exclusion step
- **V5–V6**: Must confirm absence of broad, notched R to exclude LBBB
- **aVR**: Terminal R >3mm in TCA context = highly specific TCA toxicity marker
- **All limb leads**: Assess for QRS fragmentation (jagged, notched QRS without clear BBB pattern) suggesting scar or metabolic disease

### 2.4 Beat-by-Beat Considerations

In hyperkalemia, QRS width should be tracked across serial beats — progressive widening with each beat (especially if rate is slowing) indicates worsening hyperkalemia. The P-wave may simultaneously be flattening (hyperkalaemia affects atrial conduction). In TCA overdose, the QRS may widen rapidly — serial measurement every few minutes is appropriate in the acute toxic phase.

## 3. Morphology Details

### 3.1 QRS Width

≥120ms by definition. In severe hyperkalemia (K+ >7.5 mEq/L), QRS may reach 200ms or more (sine wave). In TCA overdose, QRS >160ms correlates with increased seizure and hemodynamic collapse risk. In cardiomyopathy-related IVCD, QRS is typically 120–150ms.

### 3.2 QRS Morphology Patterns Within IVCD

**Non-specific wide QRS**: QRS duration ≥120ms with combination of right and left conduction delay features that do not predominate as either RBBB or LBBB. May look like a degraded RBBB or LBBB that fails morphologic criteria.

**QRS fragmentation**: Multiple notches, RSR' patterns without meeting full RBBB criteria, W-shaped or M-shaped QRS in leads where these are not expected. Suggests regional conduction delay from scar.

**Terminal rightward deflection**: In TCA overdose, the final 40ms of the QRS vector is directed rightward and superiorly — produces a prominent R-wave in aVR and a deep S in I. This is a specific sub-pattern within IVCD.

**Progressive QRS widening toward sine wave**: In hyperkalemia — characteristic non-BBB widening, with P-wave loss and eventual sine wave.

### 3.3 P-Wave

- In hyperkalemia: P-waves flatten and disappear (sinoventricular rhythm) before QRS becomes critically wide. Absent P-waves with wide QRS = severe hyperkalemia until proven otherwise
- In TCA: Sinus tachycardia usually present (anticholinergic effect) with wide QRS
- In cardiomyopathy: P-waves usually present; may show P-mitrale if atrial dilation present

### 3.4 PR Interval

Variable. May be prolonged in hyperkalemia (atrial conduction delay). Normal in TCA early overdose. May be prolonged in cardiomyopathy/fibrosis.

### 3.5 ST-T Changes

Secondary ST-T changes from abnormal depolarization are expected (discordant T-waves). Distinguishing primary ischemic ST changes from secondary changes in IVCD requires clinical context and serial comparison. IVCD does NOT modify STEMI criteria the way LBBB does — standard STEMI thresholds apply (LBBB-specific algorithms like Sgarbossa do not apply to IVCD).

### 3.6 QT Interval

QTc measurement is difficult in wide QRS. Use JT interval (JT = QT minus QRS duration) as a surrogate for repolarization abnormality independent of conduction delay. QT prolongation in TCA context (sodium AND potassium channel block) increases TdP risk.

### 3.7 QRS Fragmentation

Presence of fragmented QRS (fQRS — RSR' without meeting RBBB criteria, notching of R or S waves in ≥2 consecutive leads of same territory) is associated with myocardial scar. fQRS in inferior leads suggests prior inferior MI or inferior scar; fQRS in anterior leads suggests anterior territory scar.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis

### 4.1 Mimics

| Mimic | How to Distinguish |
|-------|--------------------|
| RBBB | RBBB has rSR' in V1 specifically; wide terminal S in I and V6. IVCD does not. |
| LBBB | LBBB has broad notched R in V5/V6, absent septal q in lateral leads, no S in I/aVL. IVCD does not. |
| Bifascicular block | RBBB + axis deviation = specific pattern; IVCD has no specific axis/morphology pattern |
| WPW | WPW: short PR, delta wave, secondary ST-T changes; IVCD: normal or prolonged PR, no delta wave |
| Paced rhythm | Paced rhythm: pacing spike before QRS; LBBB or RBBB morphology depending on lead placement; IVCD from pacing is secondary to paced depolarization |
| Hyperkalemia | Can PRODUCE IVCD; peaked T-waves, P-wave flattening, sine wave morphology are additional clues |
| TCA overdose | Produces IVCD; terminal R in aVR, sinus tachycardia, QTc prolongation are additional clues |

### 4.2 Coexisting Conditions

- Hyperkalemia + chronic renal failure: most common metabolic cause in ER
- TCA overdose + QTc prolongation: combined Na and K channel blockade
- Dilated cardiomyopathy: IVCD is a marker of adverse prognosis; may progress to LBBB or require CRT
- Ischemic cardiomyopathy + IVCD: substrate for VT; IVCD with fragmented QRS in coronary territory = scar marker
- Post-cardiac surgery: transient IVCD from myocardial stunning or surgical trauma to conduction tissue

## 5. STAT Classification

| Priority | Criteria |
|----------|---------|
| CRITICAL — Immediate Resuscitation | IVCD + sine wave QRS pattern (extreme hyperkalemia) |
| CRITICAL — Immediate Toxicology/ICU | IVCD + QRS >160ms + TCA overdose suspected |
| STAT — Urgent Labs | IVCD + clinical hyperkalemia risk (renal failure, K-sparing drugs, metabolic acidosis) |
| STAT | New IVCD in acute presentation (any cause — exclude reversible metabolic cause first) |
| HIGH — Urgent Cardiology | IVCD + cardiomyopathy + QRS ≥150ms (potential CRT candidate) |
| ROUTINE | Chronic, stable IVCD on serial ECGs, known cardiomyopathy, unchanged |

**Key rule for IVCD in STEMI context:** IVCD does NOT modify STEMI criteria — standard ST elevation thresholds apply. Do NOT apply Sgarbossa criteria to IVCD (those criteria are LBBB-specific). Evaluate for STEMI using standard criteria.

## 6. Reasoning Complexity Analysis

### 6.1 Reasoning Domains Required

- Pattern exclusion: confirm QRS is wide AND does not meet RBBB or LBBB criteria
- Morphology analysis: identify any sub-patterns (TCA terminal R in aVR, sine wave, fragmentation)
- Metabolic reasoning: link QRS pattern to clinical context (renal failure → hyperkalemia; overdose → sodium channel block)
- Temporal pattern: track QRS width change across serial ECGs
- Differential exclusion: systematic exclusion of all definable BBB patterns

### 6.2 Feature Dependencies

```
QRS ≥120ms?
    → Yes: proceed to morphology exclusion
V1: rSR' or rsR' present with wide S in I/V6?
    → YES → RBBB (reclassify; this is NOT IVCD)
V5/V6: Broad notched R, absent septal q, absent S in I?
    → YES → LBBB (reclassify)
Axis with bifascicular criteria?
    → YES → Bifascicular block (reclassify)
None of the above → IVCD
    → Sub-classify: terminal R aVR? → TCA pattern
    → P-waves absent + peaked T? → Hyperkalemia pattern
    → QRS fragmentation? → Scar pattern
    → Residual: non-specific cardiomyopathy/fibrosis pattern
```

### 6.3 Cross-Condition Interactions

- Hyperkalemia: T-wave peaking is an early sign (Node 2.7.X hyperkalemia file); IVCD is a later sign — alert must cross-reference T-wave analysis
- TCA overdose: sinus tachycardia + IVCD + prolonged QTc = TCA triad; requires toxicology flag
- STEMI: IVCD does NOT trigger LBBB-modified STEMI criteria — failure to recognize this causes both false-positive (applying Sgarbossa) and false-negative (thinking IVCD "masks" STEMI) errors
- AFib with aberrant conduction: wide QRS during AFib may be IVCD morphology rather than BBB aberrancy — this is a rate-dependent IVCD variant

### 6.4 Reasoning Chain Sketch

```
1. Measure QRS duration → ≥120ms?
2. Evaluate V1: rSR' present? → No → RBBB excluded
3. Evaluate V5/V6: broad notched R? → No → LBBB excluded
4. Evaluate axis and limb lead morphology → fascicular block criteria met? → No
5. Diagnosis: IVCD (non-specific)
6. Sub-pattern search:
   a. aVR terminal R >3mm + sinus tachycardia? → TCA flag
   b. P-waves absent/flat + peaked T + progressive widening? → Hyperkalemia flag
   c. QRS fragmentation? → Scar/cardiomyopathy flag
7. Assign STAT level based on cause
8. Explicitly note: standard STEMI criteria apply (not LBBB-modified)
```

### 6.5 Confidence Anchors

- High confidence: QRS ≥120ms + V1 lacks rSR' + V6 lacks broad R = IVCD confirmed
- High confidence: Terminal R aVR >3mm in suspected overdose = TCA sodium channel block pattern
- High confidence: Progressive QRS widening + P-wave loss + peaked T + known hyperkalemia = metabolic IVCD
- Low confidence: Morphologic differentiation between partial bilateral BBB disease vs. diffuse cardiomyopathy — requires clinical and imaging context

### 6.6 Difficulty Score

| Dimension | Score (1–5) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Signal clarity needed | 2 | Wide QRS is usually clearly visible; exclusion criteria are also measurable |
| Number of leads required | 3 | V1, V5/V6, aVR, and limb leads all needed for full exclusion |
| Cross-domain reasoning | 3 | Must link ECG pattern to clinical context (toxicology, metabolic, structural) |
| Temporal pattern complexity | 2 | Serial ECG comparison valuable but single ECG usually sufficient for diagnosis |
| Differential complexity | 2 | Exclusion-based logic is clear once RBBB and LBBB criteria are checked |
| Rarity in PTB-XL | 2 | Labeled in PTB-XL as IVCD or non-specific conduction delay; moderate prevalence |
| Overall difficulty | 2.5 | Diagnosis by exclusion is systematic; challenge lies in cause identification, not ECG pattern |

## 6A. Agent Assignment

**Architecture:** Option E — 3 Parallel Phase 1 Specialists + 1 Sequential CDS (Node 2.1 Gate Passed 2026-03-27)

| Agent | Role for Intraventricular Conduction Delay (IVCD) | Key Features Used |
|-------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| **RRC** (Rhythm/Rate/Conduction) | Primary | QRS duration ≥120ms confirming wide QRS; confirms QRS widening does not meet LBBB or RBBB duration-only criteria (diagnosis of exclusion for morphology) |
| **IT** (Ischemia/Territory) | Not involved | No ischemia-specific features in isolated IVCD |
| **MR** (Morphology/Repolarization) | Co-Primary | QRS morphology does NOT meet LBBB criteria (no broad monophasic R in I/V5-V6, no absent septal Q) AND does NOT meet RBBB criteria (no rSR' in V1-V2, no wide S in I/V6); non-specific wide QRS morphology; secondary ST-T changes if present |
| **CDS** (Cross-Domain Synthesis) | Standard integration only | Confirms that RRC (QRS ≥120ms) combined with MR (morphology not meeting LBBB or RBBB patterns) yields IVCD classification; determines ischemia assessment strategy — IVCD partially reduces ST interpretation reliability but does not require full Sgarbossa; notes IVCD as a non-specific finding |

### Primary Agent
**RRC and MR co-primary** — IVCD requires both a QRS duration threshold (≥120ms from RRC) and the absence of specific BBB morphology patterns (MR confirms neither LBBB nor RBBB pattern is met); both domains are required for the diagnosis of exclusion.

### Cross-Domain Hints
- RRC emits: `cross_domain_hint: "QRS ≥120ms detected — MR must confirm morphology does not meet LBBB (no broad R in I/V5-V6) or RBBB (no rSR' in V1-V2) criteria; if neither pattern met, CDS classifies as IVCD"`

### CDS Specific Role
CDS acts as the arbiter for IVCD classification. It receives RRC QRS duration (≥120ms) and MR morphology assessment, and determines that the wide QRS does not conform to LBBB or RBBB patterns — rendering an IVCD (non-specific intraventricular conduction delay) diagnosis. CDS notes that ischemia assessment in IVCD is intermediate: standard ST criteria apply with reduced specificity (neither full Sgarbossa for LBBB nor unrestricted standard criteria), and flags this limitation in the final output. No Sgarbossa application is required unless LBBB morphology is suspected.

## 9. Edge Cases and Pitfalls

**IVCD does NOT trigger LBBB-modified STEMI criteria.** The Sgarbossa criteria and modified Sgarbossa criteria were derived and validated specifically in LBBB. Applying them to IVCD has no evidence base and may produce false-positive or false-negative STEMI calls. When IVCD is present and STEMI is suspected, use standard thresholds. This is one of the highest-stakes errors possible in ER ECG interpretation.

**Hyperkalemia is the most dangerous reversible cause.** Wide QRS in a patient on dialysis, with acute kidney injury, or on potassium-sparing medications should trigger immediate potassium check. QRS widening from hyperkalemia can progress to VF or asystole within minutes. Do not wait for lab results if the clinical picture fits — treat empirically with calcium gluconate.

**TCA overdose QRS monitoring.** In TCA overdose, QRS >100ms predicts seizure risk; QRS >160ms predicts hemodynamic collapse. Serial ECG measurement every 15–30 minutes is indicated in the acute phase. The terminal R in aVR is a sensitive TCA marker but can be absent early or in some TCA variants (not all TCAs are equally cardiotoxic).

**IVCD in AFib.** When AFib produces an IVCD-morphology wide QRS, this may reflect either: (a) rate-dependent aberrancy producing non-specific conduction delay, (b) underlying IVCD unmasked by rapid rate, or (c) the start of a pre-excited AFib (WPW). Delta wave inspection and clinical context are required.

**Fragmented QRS as a standalone finding.** QRS fragmentation without meeting full IVCD criteria (QRS <120ms) can occur as a scar marker. This is a sub-threshold IVCD variant. In patients with syncope, fQRS in a coronary territory is an independent predictor of adverse cardiac events.

**Pseudo-normalization.** In a patient with prior LBBB or RBBB, acute myocardial ischemia may cause IVCD by "canceling" the prior bundle branch morphology — apparent normalization or unexpected change in QRS morphology. New QRS narrowing or morphology change in a patient with known BBB should trigger urgent evaluation, not reassurance.
