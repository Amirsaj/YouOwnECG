## 1. Pathophysiology Root Cause

### 1.1 What Goes Wrong (Anatomy/Physiology)
- The left bundle branch (LBB) is a broad, fan-like structure arising from the His bundle and dividing into the left anterior fascicle (LAF) and left posterior fascicle (LPF), plus septal fibers
- Block occurs anywhere from the His bundle itself to the proximal LBB before fascicular division — complete LBBB means BOTH fascicles are effectively blocked
- Causes: coronary artery disease (most common), hypertensive heart disease, cardiomyopathy, aortic stenosis, cardiac surgery, rarely idiopathic
- New LBBB in the context of chest pain must be treated as STEMI equivalent until proven otherwise
- Unlike RBBB which can be benign, LBBB almost always indicates structural heart disease

### 1.2 Electrical Consequence
- Normal septal activation proceeds LEFT→RIGHT (septum depolarizes from left side first via left septal fibers)
- In LBBB, the septum CANNOT be activated from the left → activation is reversed: RIGHT→LEFT via slow cell-to-cell conduction through the septum
- The left ventricle is activated late via slow conduction from the right ventricle (spreading through ventricular myocardium, not specialized conduction)
- Total QRS duration markedly prolonged (≥120 ms) due to slow cell-to-cell spread
- Repolarization is abnormal and OPPOSITE to depolarization (secondary ST-T changes) — this is the key diagnostic trap for ischemia

### 1.3 Why It Appears on ECG
- Reversed septal activation eliminates normal r wave in V1 and normal septal q wave in lateral leads (I, aVL, V5, V6)
- Late, slow LV activation produces broad, notched R waves in lateral leads
- Wide QS or rS in V1 (RV dominates, LV is late)
- Secondary ST depression and T-wave inversion in leads with dominant R — these are DISCORDANT changes (normal in LBBB, NOT ischemic)
- Primary CONCORDANT ST changes (ST elevation in leads with dominant R, or ST depression in leads with dominant S) are abnormal and indicate superimposed ischemia → Sgarbossa criteria

## 2. ECG Presentation — Lead by Lead

### 2.1 Diagnostic Criteria (2025 AHA/ESC Guidelines)

| Criterion | Threshold | Source |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| QRS duration | ≥120 ms | ESC 2022 / ACC/AHA 2025 |
| Dominant broad notched R in V5 or V6 | Present (often notched "M" shape) | AHA |
| Absent septal q waves in I, aVL, V5, V6 | q wave absent in all lateral leads | AHA |
| QS or rS complex in V1 | Present | ESC |
| No secondary R' in V1 (differentiates from RBBB) | Absent | AHA |
| ST and T wave direction opposite to main QRS deflection (discordance) | Expected finding, NOT ischemic | ESC |

**LBBB + STEMI (Sgarbossa Criteria — see also 2.7.66):**

| Criterion | Points | Sensitivity | Specificity |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Concordant ST elevation ≥1 mm in leads with positive QRS | 5 | 18% | 99% |
| Concordant ST depression ≥1 mm in V1, V2, or V3 | 3 | 25% | 96% |
| Discordant ST elevation ≥5 mm (absolute) in leads with negative QRS | 2 | 33% | 82% |
| Score ≥3 = high probability STEMI | — | — | — |

**Smith-Modified Sgarbossa (superior, use this):**
- Concordant ST elevation ≥1 mm: same as original
- Concordant ST depression ≥1 mm V1-V3: same as original
- ST/S ratio ≤−0.25 (ST elevation divided by the depth of the preceding S wave) in any lead with negative QRS: replaces the discordant ≥5 mm criterion

### 2.2 Lead-by-Lead Manifestation

| Lead | Expected Finding | Why (Vector Explanation) | Sensitivity |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|-------------|
| I | Broad notched R, no q, ST depression + T inversion (discordant) | Late LV activation produces leftward terminal vector; discordant repol | High |
| II | Variable broad R or QS; ST/T changes | Inferior-leftward lead; variable depending on axis | Moderate |
| III | Often QS or rS; ST elevation may occur (discordant) | Inferior-rightward; late LV activation appears negative | Moderate |
| aVR | Broad QS or rS; ST elevation (discordant) | aVR is inverse of lateral; elevated in discordant fashion | High |
| aVL | Broad notched R; no q; ST depression + T inversion (discordant) | Leftward axis; similar to lead I | High |
| aVF | Variable; QS or broad R depending on axis | Inferior lead; often delayed positivity | Moderate |
| V1 | QS or rS pattern (no R or tiny r); broad | Reversed septum: right→left activation makes V1 entirely negative | Very High |
| V2 | QS or rS; ST elevation (discordant — expected, not ischemic) | Right precordial: still predominantly negative deflection | Very High |
| V3 | Transitional; rS or RS | Transition zone shifts rightward in LBBB | High |
| V4 | RS or broad R beginning; ST depression (discordant) | Transition; beginning of lateral lead pattern | High |
| V5 | Broad notched R ("M" shaped), no q; ST depression + T inversion | LV lateral activation; classic LBBB morphology | Very High |
| V6 | Broad notched R, no q; ST depression + T inversion | LV lateral; mirror of V1 pattern | Very High |

### 2.3 Key Leads (Most Diagnostic)
- **V1**: QS or rS (absent R or tiny r) + broad QRS → most specific for LBBB
- **V5-V6**: Broad notched (plateau or M-shaped) R without septal q → confirms LBBB
- **I, aVL**: Broad R without q → lateral confirmation
- **V1-V3**: Concordant ST depression here (in LBBB with STEMI context) is a high-specificity Sgarbossa criterion

### 2.4 Beat-by-Beat Considerations
- LBBB is typically constant across all beats (fixed block)
- Rate-dependent LBBB: block appears only above a critical heart rate (aberrant conduction); disappears at normal rates
- Intermittent LBBB is rarer but must be recognized — rhythm strips may show alternating normal and LBBB beats
- In rate-dependent LBBB, the QRS after a long pause may be narrow while tachycardia shows LBBB morphology

## 3. Morphology Details (What the Agent Must See)

### 3.1 P-wave Changes
- Morphology: normal P waves (LBBB is infranodal — P-wave generation is unaffected)
- Duration: normal
- Axis: normal
- 1:1 P:QRS relationship preserved (unless coexisting AV node disease)

### 3.2 PR Interval Changes
- Duration: normal to mildly prolonged (LBBB itself doesn't prolong PR; if PR >200 ms, concurrent 1° AV block)
- Pattern: constant
- PR prolongation + LBBB may indicate diffuse conduction disease (trifascicular if also RBBB+LAFB pattern exists)

### 3.3 QRS Complex Changes
- Duration: ≥120 ms (complete LBBB) — typically 130-160 ms
- Morphology: broad, often notched/plateau R in V5-V6, V1; QS or rS in V1
- Amplitude: large in lateral leads (LV dominates)
- Axis: normal or mildly leftward; extreme LAD suggests concurrent LAFB

### 3.4 ST Segment Changes
- Direction: DISCORDANT ST changes are NORMAL in LBBB — ST elevation in leads with downgoing QRS (V1-V3), ST depression in leads with upgoing QRS (I, aVL, V5-V6)
- These discordant changes can be massive (ST elevation V1-V3 up to 4-5 mm) and are NOT ischemia
- CONCORDANT ST changes = ABNORMAL = must trigger Sgarbossa analysis
- Measurement: standard J-point; Sgarbossa uses J-point elevation/depression

### 3.5 T-wave Changes
- Direction: T-wave DISCORDANT to main QRS deflection — inverted in leads with tall R (I, aVL, V5-V6); upright in leads with QS (V1-V3)
- This discordant pattern is expected — do NOT diagnose ischemia based on T-wave alone in LBBB
- Wellens-type T-wave inversion CANNOT be diagnosed in the presence of LBBB
- Amplitude: variable; T-waves may be large in V1-V3 (discordant upright)
- Any CONCORDANT T-wave change (same direction as QRS) is abnormal

### 3.6 QT/QTc Changes
- QT prolonged (mandatory — wide QRS forces long QT)
- QTc calculation is unreliable in LBBB — do not diagnose long QT syndrome from QTc in LBBB context
- Bazett/Fridericia formulas are not validated for LBBB

### 3.7 Other Features
- No delta waves (LBBB is not pre-excitation; initial QRS deflection is not slurred in the typical delta-wave fashion)
- In some cases, the notching in R wave gives an "M" or plateau appearance in V5-V6
- Concordance rule: if ST segment goes in SAME direction as QRS, that is pathological
- Loss of normal precordial R-wave progression (V1→V6 should show increasing R; in LBBB, V1-V3 may be entirely negative)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis

### 4.1 Mimics (What Looks Like This But Isn't)

| Mimic Condition | Shared Features | Distinguishing Features |
|----------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| RBBB | Wide QRS (≥120 ms), abnormal morphology | RBBB: rSR' in V1 (not QS); wide S in I and V6 (not absent q); no notched R in V5-V6 |
| Hyperkalemia | Wide QRS, sine wave pattern | Hyperkalemia: no typical lateral R; peaked T waves; history; gradual widening |
| Pre-excitation (WPW) | Wide QRS, abnormal initial deflection | WPW: short PR, delta wave present; QRS starts early (pre-excited), not ending late |
| IVCD (nonspecific) | Wide QRS | IVCD: doesn't meet full LBBB criteria; may have partial features |
| Ventricular paced rhythm | Wide QRS, LBBB morphology from RV lead | Pacing: pacemaker spikes before each QRS; right bundle territory pacing creates LBBB pattern — clinically identical |
| Ventricular tachycardia | Wide QRS, bizarre morphology | VT: dissociated P waves (AV dissociation); fusion/capture beats; Brugada/Vereckei criteria |

### 4.2 Coexisting Conditions
- **LBBB + STEMI**: LBBB masks STEMI — ST changes from LBBB (discordant) are the expected "normal" appearance; STEMI creates CONCORDANT changes on top of this → Sgarbossa criteria required
- **LBBB + Anterior MI**: Loss of R in V1-V4 (expected in LBBB anyway) — OLD anterior MI CANNOT be diagnosed from pathological Q waves in the presence of LBBB
- **LBBB + LVH**: LVH criteria are unreliable in LBBB — voltage is altered by the conduction defect
- **LBBB + Wellens**: Cannot diagnose Wellens T-wave changes within LBBB
- **Rate-dependent LBBB**: Appears only at fast rates — appears as aberrant conduction during SVT or with increased sinus rate; must distinguish from VT

## 5. STAT Classification

| Priority | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| **STAT** | NEW LBBB in the setting of chest pain, dyspnea, or hemodynamic compromise = STEMI equivalent — cath lab activation required until proven otherwise |
| **Time-sensitive** | New LBBB: treat as STEMI, door-to-balloon time applies; Sgarbossa analysis should be completed within the same timeframe as STEMI workup |
| **Clinical action** | (1) Determine if LBBB is new vs old (compare to prior ECGs) — old LBBB with new symptoms requires Sgarbossa; (2) Apply Smith-modified Sgarbossa if any concern for ischemia; (3) Any concordant ST change → emergent cardiology consult; (4) Do NOT be falsely reassured by discordant ST elevation in V1-V3 — this is expected in LBBB |

### 6.1 Reasoning Domains Required to Detect This Condition
- **Interval measurement**: QRS duration ≥120 ms (primary criterion)
- **Morphology analysis**: QS/rS in V1; broad notched R in V5-V6; absent q in lateral leads
- **Lead-group correlation**: Consistent pattern across all 12 leads (cannot be diagnosed from subset)
- **Discordance analysis**: Determine whether each lead's ST/T is concordant or discordant with QRS — fundamental to every interpretation decision in LBBB
- **Ischemia detection**: If ANY concordant ST change → Sgarbossa analysis required
- **Sgarbossa/Smith ratio calculation**: ST/S ratio requires measuring both ST elevation AND S wave depth in the same beat
- **Temporal context**: Is this LBBB new (ischemia until proven otherwise) vs old (apply Sgarbossa)?
- **Rate dependency detection**: Does the block appear only at certain heart rates?
- LBBB requires ALL of these domains — highest cross-domain requirement of any conduction disorder

### 6.2 Feature Dependencies
- **ESSENTIAL features from SDA-1**:
  - QRS duration per beat (milliseconds, not just "wide/narrow")
  - V1 morphology: QS vs rS vs rSR' (must discriminate from RBBB)
  - V5/V6 morphology: R wave notching, absence of septal q wave
  - ST-segment displacement relative to isoelectric line for each lead
  - QRS dominant deflection direction (positive/negative) for each lead
  - R-wave amplitude for Sgarbossa Smith-modified ratio (need S wave depth)
- **SUPPORTING features**:
  - QRS axis (mildly leftward expected in LBBB)
  - R-wave progression across precordials
  - T-wave direction (should be discordant — same analysis as ST)
- **Features that EXCLUDE LBBB**:
  - QRS duration <120 ms
  - rSR' morphology in V1 (indicates RBBB)
  - Present septal q waves in I, aVL, V5 (rules out LBBB)
  - Delta wave at QRS onset (indicates pre-excitation, not LBBB)
- **Per-beat analysis required**: Yes — rate-dependent LBBB may appear on some beats and not others; Sgarbossa analysis requires per-beat ST measurement

### 6.3 Cross-Condition Interactions
- **LBBB MASKS**: Anterior STEMI, inferior STEMI, pathological Q waves (old MI), Wellens syndrome, LVH, QT prolongation — virtually all other diagnoses require LBBB to be identified FIRST before proceeding
- **LBBB CHANGES INTERPRETATION**: ALL ST-T changes must be re-analyzed through the discordance framework — what looks like ischemia may be normal LBBB; what looks normal may be hidden ischemia
- **LBBB + WPW**: Cannot coexist in the same beat (delta wave rules out simple LBBB); however, LBBB pattern can be mimicked by RV pre-excitation
- **Rate-dependent LBBB + SVT**: SVT with rate-dependent LBBB must be distinguished from VT (both cause wide-complex tachycardia)
- **LBBB + 3° AV Block**: If a junctional escape pacemaker fires through an LBBB, the resulting escape rhythm shows LBBB morphology — this is the expected appearance, not VT

### 6.4 Reasoning Chain Sketch

**Minimum reasoning chain (LBBB detection)**:
1. Measure QRS duration across all beats → ≥120 ms? → Yes
2. Check V1 morphology → QS or rS (no secondary R')? → Yes
3. Check V5/V6 → broad notched R, no q wave? → Yes
4. Confirm: ST/T discordant in all leads → Expected pattern → LBBB confirmed

**Full reasoning chain (LBBB + ischemia assessment)**:
1. Detect LBBB (minimum chain above)
2. Assess ALL 12 leads for concordance:
   - For each lead: is QRS predominantly positive or negative?
   - Is ST elevated or depressed?
   - Is concordant (same direction as QRS)? → ABNORMAL → Sgarbossa
3. Sgarbossa screen:
   - Any concordant ST elevation ≥1 mm? → Score 5 → Emergency
   - Any concordant ST depression ≥1 mm in V1-V3? → Score 3 → Emergency
   - Any discordant ST elevation ≥5 mm? → Score 2
   - Smith-modified: Any lead with ST elevation and negative QRS: measure S wave depth → ST/S ratio ≤−0.25? → Emergency
4. Determine new vs old LBBB (requires comparison ECG or clinical history)
5. Integration: QRS duration + V1 morphology + lateral morphology + concordance analysis → Final diagnosis

### 6.5 Confidence Anchors
- **HIGH confidence LBBB**: QRS ≥120 ms + QS in V1 + broad notched R in V5-V6 + absent q in I/aVL/V6 + appropriate ST discordance (all criteria met)
- **LOWER confidence**: QRS 110-119 ms (may be incomplete LBBB), or isolated morphology change without duration criterion
- **LBBB + superimposed ischemia**: Any concordant ST change, especially Smith-modified ratio ≤−0.25
- **Pathognomonic combination for LBBB**: QRS ≥120 ms + V1 QS/rS + V5-V6 broad R without q + discordant ST/T (all features in combination) → near 100% specific
- **Minimum evidence for "possible LBBB"**: Wide QRS + V1 with no secondary R' wave + predominantly leftward forces
- **Minimum for "definite LBBB"**: All four major criteria met (QRS ≥120, V1 QS/rS, V5/V6 broad R, absent septal q in lateral leads)

### 6.6 Difficulty Score

| Dimension | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Signal clarity needed | 3 | Wide QRS is usually obvious; Sgarbossa ratio requires accurate ST measurement |
| Number of leads required | 5 | All 12 leads mandatory — concordance analysis requires every lead |
| Cross-domain reasoning | 5 | Must simultaneously analyze morphology, interval, ST concordance, ischemia context |
| Temporal pattern complexity | 3 | Fixed LBBB is constant; rate-dependent requires rhythm analysis |
| Differential complexity | 5 | Must exclude RBBB, VT, WPW, hyperkalemia; must assess for superimposed ischemia |

## 6A. Agent Assignment

**Architecture:** Option E — 3 Parallel Phase 1 Specialists + 1 Sequential CDS (Node 2.1 Gate Passed 2026-03-27)

| Agent | Role for Left Bundle Branch Block | Key Features Used |
|-------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| **RRC** (Rhythm/Rate/Conduction) | Co-Primary | QRS duration ≥120ms; PR interval (prolonged PR + LBBB raises trifascicular concern); rate context; new vs chronic determination |
| **IT** (Ischemia/Territory) | Critical Supporting — MUST NOT apply standard ST criteria in LBBB | Flags any ischemic concern present (symptoms, clinical context, or paradoxical ST findings); emits cross_domain_hint to CDS for Sgarbossa application; does NOT independently classify ST changes as ischemic in the presence of LBBB |
| **MR** (Morphology/Repolarization) | Co-Primary | Broad monophasic R in leads I, aVL, V5-V6 (≥120ms); absent septal Q waves in I/V5-V6; QS or rS pattern in V1-V3; secondary discordant ST-T changes (ST depression and T inversion in leads with dominant R; ST elevation and upright T in leads with QS/rS) |
| **CDS** (Cross-Domain Synthesis) | Required — CRITICAL Sgarbossa application | Receives LBBB confirmation from RRC + MR; applies Sgarbossa criteria when any ischemic concern is flagged: concordant ST elevation ≥1mm (any lead), concordant ST depression ≥1mm in V1-V3, excessive discordant ST elevation ≥25% of preceding S-wave depth (Smith-modified Sgarbossa); generates final ischemia determination |

### Primary Agent
**RRC and MR co-primary** — LBBB requires QRS duration ≥120ms (RRC) confirmed alongside broad R in I/aVL/V5-V6 with absent septal Q and QS in V1-V3 (MR); both domains are mandatory for diagnosis.

### Cross-Domain Hints
- RRC emits: `cross_domain_hint: "QRS ≥120ms detected — MR must confirm LBBB morphology (broad R in I/V5-V6, absent septal Q, QS in V1-V3); IT must NOT classify ST changes by standard criteria until CDS applies Sgarbossa"`
- MR emits: `cross_domain_hint: "LBBB morphology confirmed — all ST-T changes are potentially discordant secondary changes; IT must suspend standard ischemia classification and forward all ST measurements to CDS for Sgarbossa analysis"`
- IT emits (if any ischemic signal detected): `cross_domain_hint: "Ischemic concern present in LBBB context — CDS must apply Sgarbossa criteria: check concordant STE ≥1mm, concordant STD ≥1mm in V1-V3, and Smith-modified Sgarbossa discordant STE ≥25% of S-wave depth"`

### CDS Specific Role
CDS is the critical integration point for LBBB. After confirming LBBB from RRC (QRS ≥120ms) and MR (broad R in I/aVL/V5-V6, absent septal Q, QS/rS in V1-V3), CDS takes over all ischemia determination. It applies Sgarbossa criteria: (1) concordant ST elevation ≥1mm in any lead (5 points — most specific), (2) concordant ST depression ≥1mm in V1, V2, or V3 (3 points), (3) excessive discordant ST elevation where STE exceeds 25% of the preceding S-wave depth by the Smith-modified formula (2 points; score ≥3 = positive). New LBBB without prior ECG for comparison is treated as a STAT equivalent and CDS generates an immediate escalation flag regardless of Sgarbossa score. CDS also integrates RRC PR interval: LBBB + prolonged PR documents trifascicular disease risk.

## 9. Edge Cases and Pitfalls

- **The Greatest Pitfall**: Falsely reassuring discordant ST elevation in V1-V3 — in LBBB, ST elevation in V1-V3 is EXPECTED (discordant) and does NOT indicate ischemia; misinterpreting this as "not STEMI" when there is concordant ST in other leads
- **New vs Old**: A new LBBB in chest pain is STEMI equivalent; an old LBBB with new symptoms still requires Sgarbossa analysis but may not trigger automatic cath lab activation — clinical correlation essential
- **Sgarbossa Insensitivity**: Original Sgarbossa criteria have poor sensitivity (~36-50%) — a negative Sgarbossa does NOT rule out STEMI in LBBB; clinical context, troponin, and echocardiography are required
- **Smith-Modified Superiority**: The ST/S ratio ≤−0.25 is more sensitive and should be used preferentially over absolute 5 mm threshold
- **Rate-Dependent LBBB**: In a long rhythm strip, LBBB may be intermittent — a single 10-second ECG may capture normal beats AND LBBB beats
- **Paced LBBB Pattern**: Right ventricular pacing creates LBBB morphology — pacemaker spikes before QRS are the key discriminator; Sgarbossa criteria still apply to paced rhythms (modified Sgarbossa)
- **LBBB in Women**: Women have narrower QRS at baseline; QRS 115-120 ms in a woman with cardiac symptoms may warrant evaluation despite not strictly meeting criteria
- **Hyperkalemia Mimic**: Severe hyperkalemia causes progressive QRS widening that can simulate LBBB — check serum K+ and look for peaked T waves, history of renal failure
